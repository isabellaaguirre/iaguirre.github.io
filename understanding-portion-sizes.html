Why Portion Sizes Matter

Eating appropriate portion sizes is essential for maintaining a healthy diet and avoiding overeating. Over time, portion sizes have increased, leading to higher calorie intake and potential weight gain.

Portion vs. Serving Size

  • Serving size: The amount of food recommended on a nutrition label.
  • Portion size: The amount of food you choose to eat, which may be larger or smaller than a serving size.

General Portion Size Guidelines

Here are some easy ways to estimate portion sizes using everyday objects:

  • Protein (Meat, Fish, Poultry): A portion should be about the size of your palm (3-4 ounces).
  • Carbohydrates (Pasta, Rice, Bread): A portion should be about the size of a fist (½ cup cooked pasta or rice, or one slice of bread).
  • Vegetables: Fill half your plate with vegetables (about two fists).
  • Fats (Butter, Oils, Nuts): A portion is about the size of your thumb (one tablespoon).
  • Cheese: A portion should be about the size of four dice (one ounce).

Tips for Better Portion Control

  • Use smaller plates to help control portion sizes.
  • Measure food with cups or a food scale to avoid overeating.
  • Eat slowly and listen to your body's hunger cues.
  • When dining out, ask for a half portion or pack half of your meal to take home.
  • Pre-portion snacks into small containers instead of eating from the bag.
